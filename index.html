The Fight Against Food Deserts

Ever notice living miles away from a supermarket? If you answered yes, you are living in a food desert. The term refers to people who have limited access to a variety of healthy food options. These regions often feature large proportions of households with low incomes and inadequate access to transportation, according to the U.S Department of Agriculture. A decade ago, the department researched and found "23.5 million people live in low-income areas that are further than 1 mile from a large grocery store or supermarket," and the New York Times found 750,000 residents living in food deserts. Here are ways to access healthy food options near you:

Community Gardens

Community gardens provide the community with fresh produce. Each garden is different and may provide resources to enjoy a healthier lifestyle. The produce is grown on a smaller scale but allows people to bring home healthier options. The socially driven project attempts to create an aestically pleasing environment and increase the sense of community with residents. These gardens combat food deserts as fresh products are offered for consumption. Click here to find more information on community gardens in New York.

Community Fridges

Community fridges are a way to combat food deserts and food insecurity. Maintained by volunteers, community members, or local businesses, these fridges provide residents with various food options. These fridges are placed in a public areas and help residents who have difficulty accessing grocery stores. Anyone can access these fridges for free, and people are allowed to take what they need or leave what they can. Community fridges offer free food, from fresh produce to home-cooked meals, along with sanitary products and other health-related supplies to communities. Click here to find a New York community fridge near you.

Farmers' Markets

New York is home to more than 400 farmers' markets, 250 farm stands and 10 mobile markets, according to the New York Department of Agriculture. The markets promote nutritional alternatives as well as help increase the consumption of locally grown fresh fruits and vegetables. Through these markets, a plethora of healthy food options is offered to the community. Plus, the food prices at farmers' markets are to be found to be cheaper or equal to grocery stores, according to the Huffington Post. Click here to find a 2021 map of Farmers' Markets in NYC.

There are several health risks that people living in food deserts are exposed to. They have a greater risk of diet-related conditions, like obesity, diabetes and cardiovascular disease. A healthy diet should include a variety of fruits and vegetables, whole grains, protein, and dairy, which can be difficult to achieve for those living in food deserts. The lack of healthy options can contribute to conditions like high blood pressure. Community gardens, community fridges and farmers' markets are feasible alternatives to gain access to healthy food options.
